🎉 Math Self-Check! 🎉

Hi there! Let’s learn how to check what you already know about math. We’ll use a fun example! Imagine you’re solving this puzzle:

2x + 3 = 15

Don't worry if you’re not sure about everything yet. This is how we learn! 😊

What You Need to Know 🧠

  • Facts: The "what" – things like numbers and math words.
  • Strategies: The "how" – the steps to solve the puzzle.
  • Steps: The exact moves to solve it.
  • Reasons: The "why" – why your steps work.

Let’s Break It Down! 🔍

Facts (The "What")

Variable: A mystery number, like "x".
Constant: A plain number, like "3".
Coefficient: A number multiplied by a mystery number, like "2x".
Equation: A math sentence with an equals sign, like "2x + 3 = 15".
Expression: A part of an equation without an equals sign.

Strategies (The "How")

Use the reverse order of math: SADMEP (Subtraction, Addition, Division, Multiplication, Exponents, Parentheses). It's like working backward to solve the puzzle!

Steps (The Exact Moves)

Additive Inverse: Subtract or add to get rid of a number.
Multiplicative Inverse: Divide or multiply to make "x" all by itself!

Reasons (The "Why")

Subtraction Rule: If you take away the same number from both sides, it’s still fair. 🧮
Division Rule: If you divide both sides by the same number, it’s still equal. ✂️

You’re Amazing! 🌟

Now, look at what you know! Are there any steps or ideas you want to learn more about? That’s how you get even better at solving puzzles like this! 🎯
